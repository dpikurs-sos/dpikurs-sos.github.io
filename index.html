About SOS Kilkenny

Values Statement

We all want the same thing: to be fulfilled, to make a positive impact, to be appreciated for who we are.
By being informed and by making bold decisions, SOS Kilkenny will hold people up, not hold them back.

Our Values Statement is inspired by the values of dignity, diversity, inclusion and self-determination.

SOS Kilkenny is a trusted provider of supports for adults with intellectual disabilities and autism in Kilkenny City and County. Our dedicated team is committed to excellence, collaboration, and making a meaningful difference.

Our Supports

SOS Kilkenny offers a broad range of person-centred supports across Kilkenny City and County, supporting people to lead meaningful and connected lives. We provide community living options, short breaks away from home, and a variety of purposeful daytime supports.

Currently, we support people to live in homes in neighbourhoods across Kilkenny. Our community-based day locations offer opportunities for personal development, lifelong learning, social connection, and employment preparation for people at all stages of life.

Additionally, we operate a part-time short-break service in Kilkenny City, offering individuals the chance to experience increased independence and to build new skills and relationships in a supportive and welcoming environment

Live

We all need different supports at different times in our lives so that we can live well.

In SOS Kilkenny, we support people in homes based in different neighbourhoods throughout Kilkenny City and County.

We support people to be part of the everyday life of the local community.

Junction: The Junction is the base for our community engagement programme, supporting people to use community facilities, for example gyms, restaurants, cafes and the public bus.

The Lighthouse: The Lighthouse is our inclusive community centre for older adults supported by SOS Kilkenny and older adults from the local community. The Lighthouse hosts the Lighthouse Dementia Café on the first Tuesday for every month.

Deenview: Deenview provides support to people from Castlecomer and its surrounds to live their lives in their local area through community engagement, employment and relationship building.

We promote wellbeing among the people we support in SOS Kilkenny offering opportunities to enjoy the creative and performing arts, horticulture, sport and fitness and culinary skills.

We also provide short breaks from home for people.

Learn

In SOS Kilkenny, we believe in giving people the skills and tools to achieve their ambitions. We provide different learning opportunities for people, depending on their needs, interests and goals and encourage people to develop their confidence, skills and experience.

The Access Academy: A bespoke education programme teaching students the skills and knowledge to be successful in the workplace.

Dreamtime Studio Hub: Home of Dreamtime Radio, Ireland’s only BAI licensed radio station where all of the presenters and content creators are people with intellectual disabilities, Dreamtime Studio Hub is SOS Kilkenny’s media centre teaching broadcasting, podcasting and photography to students.

The Pod Training Café: The Pod is our training café teaching practical skills to work in the hospitality industry, including barista, customer service and till use.
